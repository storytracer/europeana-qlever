A work in the area of literature, art or science which represents unique result of creative mental activity of author and which can be perceived through senses without regard to its form, content, quality, purpose or means of representation or degree of completion, is subject to copyright. <br/>  <br/> (5) A photographic work is defined as capturing of sight through photographic technical device provided this is a result of author’s creative mental activity; no other conditions under par. 1 apply. <br/>  <br/> § 9 Free work <br/> (1) A work becomes free if: <br/> a) the term of proprietary rights according to Section 32 has expired, <br/> b) author does not have any heirs or existing heirs reject inheritance, even before expiration of proprietary rights term according to Section 32. <br/> (2) A work which becomes free is free to be used. <br/> (3) Section 13(3) and Section 60 shall not be affected by par. 1 and 2 hereof. <br/> § 13(3).
