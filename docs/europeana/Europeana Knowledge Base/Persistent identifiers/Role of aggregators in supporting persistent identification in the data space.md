# Role of aggregators in supporting persistent identification in the data space

Persistent identifiers (PIDs) play a vital role in ensuring cultural heritage resources remain identifiable, verifiable, findable and accessible across systems. As an aggregator contributing to the data space, we rely on you to help promote the benefits of the adoption of PIDs and to support their dissemination by including existing PIDs in the metadata you provide.

# Creation of persistent identifiers

Many aggregators have asked whether they should create PIDs for aggregated resources. Our position is that cultural heritage institutions are best placed to assign PIDs to their own resources, as they are the authoritative source. If a cultural heritage institution has already assigned a PID, we strongly encourage aggregators to include it in the metadata and even taken advantage of it internally rather than creating a new PID. When sharing in the data space, this PID must be provisioned using the [Europeana Data Model (EDM) profile for PIDs](https://europeana.atlassian.net/wiki/x/BQC7ww).

> [!TIP]
> By disseminating existing PID, you:
>
> - Promote continuous access to the identified resource
> - Help respect and preserve PIDs already assigned by cultural heritage institution.
> - Contribute to a trustworthy data space.

# Aggregators' key roles

Aggregators are well positioned to influence how PIDs are adopted and implemented across the cultural heritage sector. Through your relationship with data providers and your role in shaping metadata practices, you can make sure that PIDs are used and disseminated in the data space:

> [!TIP]
> **Advocating for PID adoption**

As an aggregator, you should actively advocate for their adoption by raising awareness about the benefits of PIDs, such as stable referencing, improved data interoperability and long-term resource accessibility. By encouraging your data providers and partners to implement and use PIDs, you can help strengthen the whole data space.

> [!TIP]
> **Promoting good PID practices**

Although there is broad agreement on what makes a good persistent identifier, implementations often fall short. The [Policy for persistent identifiers in the data space](https://europeana.atlassian.net/wiki/x/A4Clwg) provides twenty principles to help ensure consistent and effective PID implementation while also allowing organisations to adopt solutions based on their own technical capabilities, priorities and constraints. Data providers who adopt PIDs should be encouraged by aggregators to align their own practices with these principles.

> [!TIP]
> **Including PIDs in metadata**

If PIDs are available for aggregated resources, they should be included in the metadata following the [EDM profile for PID provision](https://europeana.atlassian.net/wiki/x/BQC7ww). Disseminating PIDs in EDM ensures they are visible and accessible to all users and systems interacting with the data. This is important because PIDs support consistent and reliable identification of resources, making discovery, citation and reuse easier.

> [!TIP]
> **Becoming a registration authority**

If desired, aggregators may take on a more formal role in the adoption of PIDs by becoming a registration authority (RA) within an existing PID system. While responsibilities differ across PID schemes, they typically include minting PIDs, maintaining associated metadata, and supporting the resolution process.
